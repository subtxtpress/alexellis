What this is

Texas offers a sales tax exemption on electricity, equipment, and other qualifying costs for registered "Large Data Center Projects" and "Qualifying Data Centers" under the Texas Comptroller's STAR program. The program was designed to attract cloud infrastructure investment. Instead it's become a tax shelter for an increasingly diverse set of operators — including cryptocurrency miners, foreign sovereign wealth vehicles, and companies whose beneficial owner is deliberately obscured behind a chain of LLCs.

This database tracks every facility registered as of early 2026. The goal is to answer questions the Comptroller's registry doesn't: Who actually controls these facilities? How much power are they drawing from ERCOT? What water are they consuming in already water-stressed counties? What did local governments give away in additional abatements to land them?

This is an active investigation. 92 of 99 facility addresses have been confirmed — 7 remain unresolved (suite addresses that appear to be registered agent offices, and road-level locations without street numbers). FOIA requests are pending. If you have documents, tips, or corrections — especially on West Texas facilities and ERCOT curtailment agreements — reach out.

Owner ≠ Occupant ≠ Operator Facilities where the registered entities don't match — a key indicator of beneficial ownership obscuring

The Comptroller registry lists three separate entity roles for each data center: owner, occupant, and operator. When all three are the same company, the record is straightforward. When they differ — especially when the occupant is an LLC with no public footprint — it usually means the beneficial user is deliberately separated from the registered exemption holder. Methodology & Sources

Primary Source

All facility data originates from the Texas Comptroller's STAR (Sales Tax And Revenue) database of registered qualifying data centers and large data center projects. The registry is public but not easily machine-readable — it was manually compiled from Comptroller records. Two facilities — Giga Texas (Tesla) and Hockley Data Center (SF HOUH LLC) — each have two registry entries: an expired registration immediately followed by a same-day renewal. The Comptroller retains both records. These are counted as 99 registry entries but 97 unique facilities.

Address Research

92 of 99 registered facilities have confirmed physical addresses. Coordinates geocoded from public records, county appraisal data, and investigative research. Seven addresses remain unresolved:

  • HELO1 DC — 949 Sherry Lane, Suite 1610, Dallas: registered agent office suite, not a data hall
  • NADC — 4201 Spring Valley Road, Suite 250, Farmers Branch: Suite 250 suggests registered office, not a data hall
  • Nexus Data Centers — 1725 Hughes Landing Blvd, 11th Floor, Spring: commercial office address, not a data hall
  • Pyote Data Center — "I-20 corridor Pyote, TX": road corridor only, no street number
  • USBTC Pecos Data Center — Farm to Market Road 2119, Pecos: road-level only, no street number
  • Poolside Data Center (Pecos County I) — FM 1053, Fort Stockton: road-level only, no street number
  • Wurldwide LLC DBA Statue LLC — US-54 & Stan Roberts Sr Ave, El Paso: intersection only, no street number

Address sources used for confirmed facilities:

  • Texas Secretary of State entity filings
  • OpenCorporates entity lookups

Entity Research

Beneficial ownership was researched using Texas SOS filings, SEC EDGAR (for publicly traded operators), OpenCorporates, and published investigative reporting. Shell entity connections (Design LLC = Google, OBOE LLC = Microsoft, Winner/Ernst LLC = Meta, Wurldwide/Statue LLC = Meta) are based on cross-referencing with known entity structures from other states and published reporting. Green Chile Ventures LLC beneficial ownership remains unconfirmed.

Exemption Program Background

Texas HB 2 (2013) created the original data center exemption. HB 1750 (2023) extended it explicitly to cryptocurrency mining facilities. The exemption covers sales tax on electricity, cooling equipment, and qualified capital expenditures. There is no public disclosure requirement for power consumption or water use.
