FOIA Exemptions Reference

Federal & State ‚Ä¢ Common denial reasons

Quick reference for understanding why agencies deny requests. Use when challenging denials or predicting redactions.

Federal FOIA Exemptions (5 U.S.C. ¬ß 552(b))

(b)(1)

National Security

Classified information concerning national defense or foreign policy
Examples: Military operations, intelligence sources/methods, diplomatic cables
(b)(2)

Internal Personnel Rules

Related solely to internal personnel rules and practices
Examples: Employee parking, cafeteria menus, routine administrative matters
(b)(3)

Statutory Exemptions

Specifically exempted by another statute
Examples: Tax returns (IRC ¬ß6103), census data, grand jury materials, patent applications
(b)(4)

Trade Secrets

Trade secrets and confidential commercial/financial information
Examples: Business financial data, proprietary formulas, competitive bidding info
(b)(5)

Deliberative Process

Inter-agency or intra-agency memorandums (attorney-client, work product, deliberative)
Examples: Draft reports, legal advice, policy discussions before decisions made
(b)(6)

Personal Privacy

Personnel, medical, or similar files - clearly unwarranted invasion of privacy
Examples: Home addresses, SSNs, medical records, personnel files
(b)(7)

Law Enforcement Records

Records compiled for law enforcement purposes (6 sub-exemptions)
Examples: Ongoing investigations, confidential sources, investigative techniques
(b)(7)(A)

LE - Interference

Could reasonably interfere with enforcement proceedings
Examples: Active criminal investigations, pending prosecutions
(b)(7)(C)

LE - Privacy

Could constitute unwarranted invasion of personal privacy
Examples: Witness identities, suspect info in ongoing cases
(b)(7)(D)

LE - Sources

Could disclose confidential source identity
Examples: Informants, undercover officers, cooperating witnesses
(b)(7)(E)

LE - Techniques

Would disclose law enforcement techniques/procedures
Examples: Investigation methods, surveillance tactics, forensic procedures
(b)(7)(F)

LE - Safety

Could endanger life/physical safety of any individual
Examples: Witness protection details, informant safety
(b)(8)

Financial Institutions

Reports prepared by agencies regulating financial institutions
Examples: Bank examination reports, financial institution safety assessments
(b)(9)

Geological Information

Geological and geophysical information concerning wells
Examples: Oil/gas well data, geological surveys

Common State Open Records Exemptions

Law Enforcement

Active investigations, investigative techniques, confidential informants
Examples: Open criminal investigations, 911 call recordings (some states), officer personnel files
State References: Missouri RSMo 610.100; Indiana IC 5-14-3-4(b)(1)

Personal Privacy

Home addresses, SSNs, medical info, employee personnel matters
Examples: Residential addresses of LEOs, victim identities, employee discipline
Often analogous to federal (b)(6)

Attorney-Client Privilege

Communications with legal counsel, pending litigation strategy
Examples: Legal advice memos, litigation hold documents
Recognized in all states

Ongoing Negotiations

Contract negotiations, real estate acquisitions, labor negotiations
Examples: Union negotiation positions, pending land purchases
Becomes public after deal concluded

Security Plans

Building security, emergency response plans, infrastructure vulnerabilities
Examples: Courthouse security protocols, critical infrastructure locations
Post-9/11 expansion in many states

Closed Session Minutes

Executive session discussions of personnel, legal, or sensitive matters
Examples: Discussions of employee discipline, litigation strategy
Must meet statutory requirements

Trade Secrets

Proprietary business information submitted to government
Examples: Vendor pricing, proprietary formulas, confidential financial data
Similar to federal (b)(4)

Preliminary Drafts

Working documents before final agency action
Examples: Draft regulations, internal policy memos before adoption
Not all states recognize this exemption
