FOIA Exemptions Reference

Federal Exemptions (5 U.S.C. ¬ß 552(b))

Exemption Category Description Common Examples
(b)(1) National Security Classified information concerning national defense or foreign policy Military operations, intelligence sources/methods, diplomatic cables
(b)(2) Internal Personnel Rules Related solely to internal personnel rules and practices Employee parking, cafeteria menus, routine administrative matters
(b)(3) Statutory Exemptions Specifically exempted by another statute Tax returns (IRC ¬ß6103), census data, grand jury materials, patent applications
(b)(4) Trade Secrets Trade secrets and confidential commercial/financial information Business financial data, proprietary formulas, competitive bidding info
(b)(5) Deliberative Process Inter-agency or intra-agency memorandums (attorney-client, work product, deliberative) Draft reports, legal advice, policy discussions before decisions made
(b)(6) Personal Privacy Personnel, medical, or similar files - clearly unwarranted invasion of privacy Home addresses, SSNs, medical records, personnel files
(b)(7) Law Enforcement Records compiled for law enforcement purposes (6 sub-exemptions) Ongoing investigations, confidential sources, investigative techniques
(b)(7)(A) LE - Interference Could reasonably interfere with enforcement proceedings Active criminal investigations, pending prosecutions
(b)(7)(C) LE - Privacy Could constitute unwarranted invasion of personal privacy Witness identities, suspect info in ongoing cases
(b)(7)(D) LE - Sources Could disclose confidential source identity Informants, undercover officers, cooperating witnesses
(b)(7)(E) LE - Techniques Would disclose law enforcement techniques/procedures Investigation methods, surveillance tactics, forensic procedures
(b)(7)(F) LE - Safety Could endanger life/physical safety of any individual Witness protection details, informant safety
(b)(8) Financial Institutions Reports prepared by agencies regulating financial institutions Bank examination reports, financial institution safety assessments
(b)(9) Geological Information Geological and geophysical information concerning wells Oil/gas well data, geological surveys

Appeal Strategies by Exemption

For (b)(5) - Deliberative Process (Most Abused)

  • Factual material is segregable and must be released
  • Final agency policy is not deliberative
  • Decision has been made; no longer predecisional
  • Privilege waived if disclosed to outsiders
  • Cite foreseeable harm standard (post-2016 FOIA Improvement Act)

For (b)(6) - Personal Privacy (Frequently Misapplied)

  • Public officials have diminished privacy expectations
  • Public interest in oversight outweighs privacy
  • Information already public or widely known
  • Names can be redacted while releasing substantive records
  • Balance test: must be "clearly unwarranted" invasion

For (b)(7) - Law Enforcement (Broadly Invoked)

  • Investigation is closed; no ongoing proceeding
  • Agency must demonstrate specific, foreseeable harm
  • Techniques/procedures are publicly known
  • Blanket withholding violates segregability requirement
  • Challenge "ongoing" claim - request status update on investigation

For (b)(7)(C) - Most Common Law Enforcement Sub-Exemption

  • Balance test required - not automatic
  • Public officials involved in misconduct have reduced privacy
  • Categorical withholding insufficient - must assess each document
  • Request redacted version with names/identifying info removed

General Strategies

  • Request Vaughn index - detailed justification for each withholding
  • Challenge blanket/categorical claims - require document-by-document review
  • Demand segregable portions per 5 U.S.C. ¬ß 552(b) - non-exempt parts must be released
  • Cite specific case law in your jurisdiction
  • Foreseeable harm standard - agency must show specific harm, not theoretical
  • Public interest balancing - emphasize oversight, accountability, public concern
